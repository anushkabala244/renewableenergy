How Solar Energy Works

Solar panels contain photovoltaic (PV) cells that convert sunlight into electricity. These cells generate direct current (DC) power, which is then converted to alternating current (AC) power by an inverter, making it usable for homes and businesses.

  • Sunlight absorption: Solar panels capture solar radiation.
  • Electricity generation: PV cells convert sunlight into electrical energy.
  • Power conversion: Inverters change DC power to AC power for home and industrial use.

Applications of Solar Energy

Residential Use

Homeowners use solar panels to generate electricity, reducing their dependency on the grid.

Industrial & Commercial Use

Businesses and industries use large-scale solar power to cut energy costs.

Rural Electrification

Solar panels provide power in remote areas where electricity grids are unavailable.
