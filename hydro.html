Harnessing the Power of Water

Hydroelectric energy is a renewable source of power that generates electricity from flowing water.

How Does Hydro Energy Work?

Hydroelectric power plants use the kinetic energy of flowing or falling water to turn turbines, which then generate electricity.

  • Water flows from a dam or river.
  • The force of the water turns a turbine.
  • The turbine rotates a generator, producing electricity.
  • The generated power is distributed through the grid.

Benefits of Hydro Energy

Renewable

Hydropower relies on the natural water cycle, making it sustainable.

Reliable

Unlike solar and wind energy, hydro energy provides a constant power supply.

Eco-Friendly

Hydropower produces no direct greenhouse gas emissions.

Advantages of Hydro Energy

  • Reduces dependence on fossil fuels.
  • Creates job opportunities in the renewable energy sector.
  • Offers flood control and water storage benefits.
  • Provides energy security and grid stability.
